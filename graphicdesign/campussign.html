Directional Messages at UC Davis

in this project we were tasked to

design and build a full-size, freestanding sign prototype

for a randomly assigned wayfinding hub on the UC Davis central campus. It should either provide orientation or direct people to at least three locations, and each typographic message should be accompanied by an arrow and pictogram.

Program

Photoshop, Illustrator, Procreate

Team

Beverly Fan, Sarah Grimes, Manreet Randhawa

Location & Message

For the first part of our project, our team was randomly assigned a location. Our group was tasked to work with the hub at the intersection of A St. and 3rd St.: the street dividing the UC Davis central campus from downtown Davis. We visited the site to identify the audience and research the needs our directional sign should fulfill. We considered whether the area consisted of vehicular or pedestrian traffic, and what kind of information and types of directional messages would be helpful at this hub.

concept

Next, we began sketching ideas for our sign. It needed to provide orientation or direct people to at least three different locations, with the inclusion of arrowa and pictograms. Additionally, we neded to consider an appropriate typeface style that followed ADA guidelines.

After discussion, we decided to create a sign. However, we soon realized that this location was difficult to place a sign, as it was only viewable from one angle: this would make it easy to follow from people coming from the campus side, but not for people approaching from any other street. We reconsidered our idea, instead deciding to expand on another idea: a three-paneled sign that were placed in the shape of a triangular prism. That way, the sign could be viewable by people coming from all angles, and would only have to contain information that were in the directions they were heading.

After, we selected and refined our sign, considering the grid, constrast, legibility, color, form, and scale of elements. We then built a preliminary scale model (1 in. to 1 ft.) of our solution for class review.

Construction

Lastly, we needed to render our final sign design for output at actual size. We printed out the panels of our sign and glued them to cardboard, attaching them to wooden stakes using velcro strips.

We then visited each location as a class, placing our full-size sign prototypes at our predetermined placement location for evaluation.
