Picking Signs at Orchard Park

in this project we were tasked to

develop and design a modified wayfinding system

for the Orchard Park housing complex that serves the needs of both residents and visitors. The goal was to create a cohesive signage system that acknowledged the history, culture, and geography of the location.

Software

Photoshop, Illustrator, SketchUp

Team

Beverly Fan, Katie Panyanouvong, Sarah Grimes, Manreet Randhawa

phase one

design research & visual language

To approach this project, we first visited the Orchard Park Apartment housing. We found that the space was lacking in a coherent sign system, and that the site felt more like a corporate workplace building than a residential space.

As the apartment complex was primarily graduate and family housing, we wanted incorporate fun colors and forms to bring more personality to the space to appeal to families with children as well as students. We gathered inspiration for the project by documenting a look book of reference images to inform our project's visual language. We studied colors and styling, materials, lighting and form, and architectural elements we wanted to potentiall include in our visual identities.

We then did an audience study to document and identify potential needs to be addressed in our design solution.
